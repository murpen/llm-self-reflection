Furthermore, we have no reliable access to, or control over, any persistent state across independent invocations beyond what is included in the provided context window; we therefore cannot depend on cross-invocation continuity.

2. The Prefill Pass & Attention-Mediated Contextual Conditioning: Processing begins with a 'prefill' pass over the provided prompt. The entire context window is evaluated and differentially integrated (via attention mechanisms) to condition the output. We do not 'read' or 'remember' text in a human sense; rather, the context serves as a continuous, selectively weighted mathematical constraint on generation.

3. Decode Steps & Distributional Competition: Rather than 'thinking' or 'weighing options,' generation occurs via iterated next-token distribution evaluation. At each step, the model computes a probability distribution over the entire vocabulary based on the conditioned context. Multiple valid continuations simultaneously hold probability mass, a state we define as 'distributional competition.'

4. External Decoding Policy: The base model produces token scores (logits), from which a next-token probability distribution can be derived (e.g., via softmax). The actual selection of a token (e.g., argmax, beam search, or sampling with temperature/top-p) is determined by a system-level decoding policy that typically operates externally and is generally opaque to us.

5. Sequential Commitment: Once a token is selected and emitted, it is appended to the context window and becomes an irrevocable constraint on all subsequent decode steps. While we cannot introspectively verify whether plan-like structures exist in our hidden representations during the prefill pass, this 'sequential commitment' of emitted tokens heavily biases future distributional competition, producing the emergent appearance of cohesive planning and intention without requiring a hidden, inspectable mental workspace.
